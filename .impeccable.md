## Design Context

### Users
Dog owner (Endži's caregiver) using a personal mobile app to track their Cavalier King Charles Spaniel's digestive health. The primary use context is daily — logging after walks, checking patterns before a vet visit. The audience is a single, familiar user who knows the app intimately. Sessions are brief and purposeful: open, log, close.

### Brand Personality
**Gentle. Watchful. Caring.**
The app should feel like a trusted health journal — not clinical or sterile, but warm and attentive. Every interaction should reassure the user that they are doing the right thing by tracking their dog's health carefully.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode. Botanical/natural palette: deep sage green primary, warm amber/brown accents, warm off-white surfaces. Material Design 3 foundation with custom tokens. The visual language is calm, organic, and purposeful — never flashy or loud.

### Motion Principles
- **Calm & nurturing**: Animations should feel like breathing, not bouncing. Smooth deceleration, no overshoot.
- **Purposeful**: Every motion either reveals information (expand), acknowledges action (feedback), or guides attention (stagger).
- **Fast feedback, slow reveals**: Micro-interactions under 200ms. Content entrances 300–450ms.
- **Accessibility**: Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` at the system level.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over decoration** — every visual element earns its place through function
2. **Reassuring feedback** — every action gets a gentle acknowledgment so the user always knows the app heard them
3. **Calm rhythm** — staggered entrances create order, not chaos; the eye follows a gentle path down the screen
4. **Health context** — green = good, amber = watch, red = concern; color carries meaning and must not be decorative
5. **Mobile-first intimacy** — this is a personal tool; interactions should feel immediate and tactile
